About

Oprah Gail Winfrey, known as Oprah Winfrey or monymonously as Oprah, is an American talk show host, television producer, actress and author. She is best known for her talk show, The Oprah Winfrey Show.

Oprah was born in Mississpi to her single teenage mother, and later grew up in Milwaukee. She faced sexual abuse as a child and in her early teenage years. She then was sent to live with father who provided her with guidance, rules and learning.

Oprah was an excellent student. she landed her first job as a co-anchor on a radio station while still in high school. She launched her own show after boosting a third-rated local Chicago show to first place.

Today, Oprah is one of the wealthiest woman in the world. She is also the highest paid entertainer in the world. She is arguably the world's most influential woman. She is also listed by Life as the most influential person of her generation. She is one of the most admired women in the world.

Achievements & Awards

Oprah is the first African-American woman on Forbes' List of Billionaires.

Oprah is one of the most influential people of her time.

In 2013, Oprah received the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

Oprah's "O Magazine" is the most successful start-up in the history of the industry.

In 1998, Oprah received the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Daytime Emmy Awards.

Oprah invested $40 million in establishing the Oprah Winfrey's Leadership Academy for Girls in Johannesburg, South Africa.

In 2011, Oprah was awarded the Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award.
